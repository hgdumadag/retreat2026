Day 1 Pulse

God, surprise me

Thursday opened with a prayer that set the tone for the whole day: God, surprise me. It felt like an invitation to stop managing the retreat and let grace arrive on its own terms.

Beneath the schedule, the stronger thread was presence. God is not waiting at the end of the struggle. He is already with you inside it.

First Talk

Fr. Albert: letting God find you

Fr. Albert's talk landed differently for each of you, but both reflections kept returning to the same truth: God does not wait to be discovered from afar. He comes near, seeks, and loves first.

Husband

How God finds me

One distinction stayed with you immediately: Christianity tells the story of how God finds us, while many other religions begin with how we find God. Emmanuel means God with us, not God kept at a distance.

The Woman at the Well became the image for that pursuit. She kept putting up walls, and Jesus kept breaking them down one by one. The question that remained with you was more personal: what am I thirsty for that never fully satisfies?

  • Recognition through serving and being noticed
  • Performing well in running, swimming, and projects
  • Getting better at AI, scripture, and catechism
  • Certifications, the gym, websites, and visible growth
  • Financial freedom and the security it seems to offer

The Ex-Presso story turned that reflection toward presence: being present for others and allowing people to change, soften, and feel transformed. God is always present, and the love that truly satisfies has already found you.

We need to allow God to find us. We need to be vulnerable to His grace and mercy and stop controlling the relationship. Let God take control of it. Let's just take a ride in His mercy and goodness and surrender ourselves.

Wife

Real love comes from God alone

For her, the first talk centered on love: we do not need to settle for love that is less than real love, because the kind that truly satisfies can only come from God.

We deserve to be loved because the truth is that we are God's beloved.

That truth became even more tender in the way she heard the Gospel: God did not stay in heaven waiting for us to climb our way back. He chose to leave heaven, seek us, and bring us home.

First Talk Images

Fr. Albert's coffee-table parable of grace and presence

These images belong with the first talk itself: the visual line that stayed with you and the onstage setup that made the message feel close, lived, and human.

Scripture Meditation

The Garden of Gethsemane

In the meditation, both of you were drawn into the same garden, but from different doors: yours through God's patient companionship in struggle, and hers through honesty, sorrow, and staying with Jesus in His suffering.

Husband

God stays with me in the places I resist

In the places that I am struggling right now, in the places that I don’t want to be, God is with me, patiently going through it with me, not rushing to pass them by but taking as much time as I choose to stay, as much time until I realize His lessons and love.

When I feel alone, ashamed and broken God is right there beside me. Just like when I was there for Geordan when he was crying, being there embracing him, comforting him and wishing that I could take his place, God is beside me loving me in ways more than anything can give my child.

For you, Gethsemane became the place where God does not demand quick resolution. He remains beside you in the places you would rather escape, staying long enough for love to do its work.

Wife

Staying with Jesus in sorrow and wounds

God affirmed her once more that it is all right to acknowledge negative feelings and thoughts about your situation in parish and ministry. Jesus Himself felt anguish and prayed honestly to the Father in the garden.

I choose to continue to be honest with God the Father and bring to Him all my thoughts and emotions and to simply come as I am without pretense.

The meditation confirmed for her that in accepting and enduring sorrows, you stay with Jesus in Gethsemane instead of leaving Him to suffer alone. To endure pain in love is to remain united with Him in His passion and death.

She also heard that wounded places are not where Jesus leaves us, but where He comes closest. God is near to the brokenhearted, and Christ allowed Himself to be wounded everywhere so even the cracks in our lives could be sanctified and healed.

Second Talk

Randy: presence before programs

Randy's talk sharpened the call to simple presence. The world needs more of our presence than our programs.

The towel, basin, and water became more than Holy Thursday symbols. In your notes, the towel reads like a receipt for something already given: salvation is not something still up for negotiation.

  • The towel as a sign that grace has already been given
  • The basin and water as the posture of stooping low
  • Washing of hands and feet as service made tangible

Randy's Slides

Images that carried the call to intimate service

Randy's message became more vivid because the slides kept turning service into something concrete: touch, nearness, kneeling low, and choosing a towel over power.
